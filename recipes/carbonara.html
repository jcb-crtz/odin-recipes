Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish from Rome, known for its creamy texture and rich flavor. Made with just a few ingredients--eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta, and black pepper--it creates a velvety sauce without using cream. The heat from the pasta gently cooks the eggs, coating each strand in a silky, cheesy emulsion.

This dish is beloved for its simplicity and depth of flavor, balancing the saltiness of the pancetta with the sharpness of the cheese, and warmth of the freshly ground black pepper. Carbonara is best enjoyed fresh, served hot, and paired with a crisp white wine for a perfect bite of Roman tradition.

  • Eggs
  • Pecorino Romano chees
  • Pancetta or Guanciale
  • Eggs

Steps//

  1. Pan fry Pancetta until golden brown. Set aside and keep the Pancetta's leftover fat in the pan.
  2. Cook noodles to just under al dente, set aside.
  3. Whisk eggs with Pecorino until emulsified
  4. Return cooked pasta to pan and coat with emulsified mixture, cook noodles to al dente. Be sure to keep the heat on medium to avoid scrambling the egg mixture!
  5. Serve hot and enjoy your life!
